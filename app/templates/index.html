Carbon Footprint

Do you know how much carbon an individual emits in a given span of time? That’s exactly what carbon footprint tells us!

A carbon footprint refers to the amount of greenhouse gases, particularly carbon dioxide (CO₂), that we release into the atmosphere through our daily activities. These gases trap heat and contribute to global warming, which is causing drastic changes to our planet’s climate. But the good news is, every one of us can make changes to reduce our carbon emissions and protect the environment.

Explore the resources below to learn how your everyday choices—like how you travel, what you eat, and the energy you consume—impact the planet, and discover practical ways to reduce your footprint.

Impacts of Climate Change

Do you know the sixth mass extinction has started?

About Mass Extinction: A mass extinction is when at least half of all species die out in a relatively short period of time. This is usually defined as about 75% of the world's species being lost in less than 2.8 million years.

History: Earth has experienced five mass extinctions in the past, including the one that wiped out the dinosaurs 65.5 million years ago. The largest mass extinction event happened about 250 million years ago, when perhaps 95 percent of all species went extinct.

Causes: Mass extinctions can be caused by a wide range of factors, including volcanic cataclysms and climate cooling.

Recovery: It can take millions of years for ecosystems to recover from a mass extinction.

Do you know 54% of India faces High to Extremely High Water Stress.

Reducing water availability impacts the agriculture sector. Agriculture sector is the backbone of many developing countries like India, Pakistan. This leads to lower crop yields, due to which layman faces problems like high food prices. The World Resource Institute (WRI) has categorized nine states and union territories in India as regions of “extremely high” water stress. Chandigarh is the most water-stressed, followed by Haryana, Rajasthan, and Uttar Pradesh. Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Bangalore, and Gujarat are also included in this list.

Do you know, raising temperature can be the reason for the next pandemic, that can be even more dangerous.

Due to increased temperature, glaciers continue to melt at unprecedented rates. Scientists have identified 1,7000 virus remnants trapped in the glaciers of western China. Scientists warn this could be a "recipe for disaster," as long-dormant viruses may spill into the environment.

In 2016, a deadly anthrax outbreak in Siberia, triggered by melting permafrost, killed a child and infected several others.

In 2014, French researchers revived a 30,000-year-old virus from Siberian permafrost that became infectious again.
